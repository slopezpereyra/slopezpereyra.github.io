It is with great enthusiasm that I write to strongly support the promotion of
Dr. Goldschmied to the rank of Associate Professor of Psychiatry. I have had the
privilege of being mentored by Dr. Goldschmied for the past four years. As a
computer science student based in Argentina, our collaboration has been mostly
remote---yet her dedication and guidance has made an impact nothing short of
extraordinary in my academic trajectory. I am honored to provide my highest
recommendation for her promotion, as she represents the best of academic
mentorship.

Dr. Goldschmied's mentoring style is defined by her remarkable commitment to
fostering independent academic growth. Nothing illustrates this better than a
story beginning at the earliest stage of our relationship. During my second week
under her guidance, I came up with a novel way of quantifying cortical
excitability with transcranial magnetic stimulation to predict depression. I was
excited but nervous to share this idea with her: it was unrelated to the
tasks she had assigned me, and why would she listen to the idea of junior,
remote student? Many mentors would have redirected me back to routine tasks
without paying any attention---but not Dr. Goldschmied. She responded with
enthusiasm, validation, and genuine interest. She encouraged me to pursue the
inquiry, actively put me in contact with researchers whose specialties were
relevant to my idea, and dedicated significant time to helping refine the
methodology and draft a manuscript with the results derived from it.
When it came the time to publish the findings, recognizing how daunting the
submission process can be for a first timer, she patiently guided me through
every administrative hurdle. She even identified and help me secure the DPN
Editors’ New Investigator Waiver Award, which covered the publication costs for
the DPN Nature journal. The story I tell here in a single paragraph comprises
three and a half years of dedicated and patient mentorship, from validating my
idea at its earliest stage to helping me secure the resources to finally see it
published. And, all the way through, Dr. Goldschmied's commitment to championing
my journey was unrelenting.


Needless to say, mentoring a student located in Argentina presents particular
challenges, especially when it comes to facilitating connections. Networking is
a crucial aspect of academic life---and one difficult to develop when working
from abroad. Despite this, Dr. Goldschmied consistently found ways to integrate
me into the scientific community. Without any prompting on my part, recognizing
that my interests aligned with their profiles, she connected me with Hans van
Dongen at Washington State University and Diego R. Mazzotti  at the University
of Kansas, with both of whom I developed highly enriching professional
relations. These introductions directly resulted in my visiting Dr.
van Dongen's laboratory in May 2024 and co-authoring a paper with Dr. Mazzotti.
But to complement these direct, virtual connections with large-scale, in-person
exposure, she strongly encouraged me to submit an abstract to the SLEEP 2024
conference. When it was accepted for a poster presentation, she helped me apply
for a grant that ultimately funded my attendance. While at the conference, she
actively introduced me to established researchers whose work aligned with my own
interests. Her efforts to bridge the gap between a student in a developing
nation and the academic community of the US highlights her remarkable commitment
to equity and the success of her mentees.

However, the most compelling evidence of Dr. Goldschmied’s excellence is the
transformative impact she has had on my career. I entered her lab as an
undergraduate with no practical research experience, no scientific network, and
only a rudimentary understanding of neuroscience. She patiently guided my
education from the ground up, training me in techniques such as EEG analysis and
TMS, as well as grounding me in theoretical concepts such as intracortical
inhibition and facilitation, slow-wave activity, and the sleep homeostasis
hypothesis. It is not an overstatement to characterize her impact on my academic
trajectory as radical. I have since published my inaugural first-author paper
(with Dr. Goldschmied as senior author), presented a poster at SLEEP 2024, and
am preparing an oral presentation for SLEEP 2026. Dr. Goldschmied took an
unexperienced student with raw ambition and meticulously developed me into a
capable, published researcher. Thanks to her tireless efforts, I am now
well-positioned to apply for doctoral programs and turn what once seemed like a
distant dream into a reality.

Without a doubt, Dr. Goldschmied embodies the very best of academic mentorship.
Her remarkable ability to nurture me from a remote, international student
without experience into an engaged, published researcher speaks volumes about
her dedication, leadership, and vision. I offer my highest and most enthusiastic
recommendation for her promotion to Associate Professor of Psychiatry.

Sincerely,
