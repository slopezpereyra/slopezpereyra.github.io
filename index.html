This is a sundry collection of scientific notes and cherished intimacies. It is inconsistent and rough around the edges—a reflection of its writer—a sort of diary pertaining to intellectual inquiries or unorganized self-reflection. For years now, I have made of writing a habit, and the principal occupation of my solitude. A consequence of this is a proliferation of orphan, bastard, or unlawful writings that lack a propper place to be, that have no addressees nor friends, and yet stubbornly dream of being read albeit once. It is such proliferation that I here collect.
